Decoy Passcode: How to Open an Empty Vault Under Pressure

21 July 2026 · Safey

A decoy passcode is a second code that opens a different, empty vault instead of your real one. Both codes work. Neither is rejected. From the outside there is no way to tell that a second vault exists at all.

It exists for one situation: the moment you are asked to unlock your phone and saying no is not a realistic option.

Why a normal passcode doesn't help there

Locks are built for absence. They protect your phone when you are not holding it — lost on a train, left on a desk, taken from a bag. They work well for that.

They do nothing when you are standing right there and someone asks you to open it. A repair shop that needs the device unlocked to test it. A border desk. A relative who hands you your own phone and waits. In every one of those, refusing is either impossible or worse than complying.

The iPhone's Hidden album has the same gap, and a sharper version of it: it opens with your device passcode. Unlocking your phone is unlocking the album. There is no second layer to hold back.

What a decoy passcode actually does

You set two codes in the same app. Enter the first and your real vault opens: your photos, your documents, your passwords. Enter the second and the app opens a vault that is empty, or holds a few unremarkable things.

Three details matter more than the feature itself:

  • The app must not behave differently. No warning, no delay, no different screen. If the decoy code produces any visible difference, it is worse than useless — it announces that a real vault exists.
  • The real files should not be loaded at all. Not hidden in the interface — not read from storage. A vault that filters what it displays can still be caught out by a file browser or a backup.
  • An empty vault is suspicious. Nobody installs a vault app to store nothing. Put a handful of dull, real things in the decoy: a few screenshots, a receipt, a photo of a parking space.

Setting one up

  1. Open your vault app's passcode settings and set your real code.
  2. Set a decoy code. Make it as plausible as the first — not 0000, and not one digit different from your real one.
  3. Open the decoy vault and add a few harmless items so it looks used.
  4. Practise entering the decoy code until it is automatic. Under pressure you will not want to be thinking about which code is which.

In Safey this lives under Settings → Passcode & Decoy, and takes about a minute. It's free — most vault apps put decoy codes behind a subscription.

What it cannot do

A decoy passcode is a good answer to one narrow problem, and it is worth being clear about the edges.

  • It doesn't survive real forensics. Someone with the device, time and specialist tooling can see that encrypted data exists. The decoy defends against a person looking at your screen, not a laboratory.
  • It doesn't help if they know the app. If someone is aware the app has a decoy feature, opening an empty vault invites the obvious next question.
  • It is not a legal strategy. In some jurisdictions, deliberately concealing data from an official demand carries consequences of its own. This is a tool for everyday privacy, not for obstructing a lawful order.

The honest summary

Most privacy tools protect your phone from strangers. A decoy passcode protects it in the one situation where you have already handed it over — where the lock has been defeated by the simplest attack there is, which is asking.

It won't defeat a forensic lab. It will handle a repair counter, a border desk, or a moment where the easiest thing to do is unlock the phone and let someone see nothing worth seeing.

FAQ

What is a decoy passcode?

A second passcode that opens a different, empty vault instead of your real one. Both codes work and neither is rejected, so from the outside there is no sign that another vault exists.

Does iPhone have a built-in decoy passcode?

No. iOS has one device passcode and the Hidden album opens with it. A decoy code requires a separate vault app that supports it.

Can someone tell a decoy vault from a real one?

Not from the unlock itself — the app opens normally. What gives it away is an obviously empty vault, which is why a good decoy vault should contain a few harmless items.
