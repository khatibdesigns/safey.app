Best Photo Vault Apps for iPhone (2026)

15 July 2026 · Safey

Search "photo vault" on the App Store and you'll find dozens of apps that promise to hide your pictures. Most just tuck them behind a passcode. The few that are worth trusting do something very different — they encrypt your photos on your device. Here's how to tell them apart and choose the right one.

What separates a real vault from a hiding app

Almost any app can move photos out of your camera roll. That's not security — it's tidiness. A genuine photo vault protects your pictures even if someone gets access to the app's servers or your account. Four things matter:

  • On-device encryption. Photos should be encrypted on your iPhone before they're uploaded, so the app's own servers only ever hold scrambled data. AES-256 is the standard to look for.
  • A key that never leaves your device. The encryption key should live in your iPhone's Keychain or Secure Enclave and never be sent anywhere — not even to the app maker.
  • Its own Face ID lock. A vault that opens with its own biometric check, separate from your phone's passcode, keeps photos private even when your phone is unlocked in someone else's hands.
  • Auto-lock. The vault should re-lock the instant you leave the app, not stay open in the background.

Red flags to avoid

  • "Hidden" but not encrypted. If an app only hides photos behind a PIN, anyone with the PIN — or access to the storage — can see everything.
  • Fake calculator apps. Disguised "vault" apps are a well-known category; the disguise is the whole product, and the encryption is often an afterthought.
  • Ads and trackers. An app monetising your attention around your most private photos is the wrong incentive.

Single-purpose vs all-in-one

Many photo vaults hide only photos. But the things people want private rarely stop at pictures — screenshots of passwords, photos of ID cards, bank details and personal notes usually live on the same phone. An all-in-one vault protects all of it behind a single lock, instead of scattering copies across several apps.
